⚡ Section 01 — Physics (Main Focus)

Electrical Power
& Light Intensity

Physics is the main focus of this project. By measuring voltage (V) and current (I) with a multimeter, we calculate electrical power (P = VI) for each lamp. This determines how much light energy each plant receives. We also calculate total energy (E = Pt) to compare efficiency.

⚡

Electrical Power: P = VI

Power (Watts) = Voltage × Current. We measured both with a multimeter for each lamp. Higher wattage → brighter lamp → more light energy reaching the plant.

🔌

Resistance & Brightness

Using Ohm's Law (V = IR), different resistor values reduce current and dim the lamp. We created Low / Medium / High light intensity groups this way.

🔋

Electrical Energy: E = Pt

Total energy consumed = Power × Time (seconds). We compared energy used per group against growth rate to analyse light efficiency at the end of the experiment.

🌿 Section 02 — Biology

Transpiration &
Plant Growth

Light intensity controls transpiration through stomatal opening. This affects water uptake, cell elongation, and ultimately plant height — explaining why different lamp powers produced different growth results.

💧

Transpiration

Water evaporates from mesophyll cell walls into air spaces, then diffuses through open stomata down a concentration gradient (high inside → low outside).

🌿

Light & Stomata

Guard cells use light energy to pump K⁺ in — water follows by osmosis, making them turgid and opening the pore. More light = wider stomata = faster transpiration.

📏

Water Uptake & Growth

Faster transpiration creates tension in xylem, pulling more water up from roots. Water is needed for photosynthesis, turgor pressure, and cell elongation — all increasing plant height.

🔗

The Light → Growth Chain

More light → stomata open wider → faster transpiration → more water via xylem → more photosynthesis + cell elongation → greater plant height. Write your explanation of how your results support this chain.

⚗️ Section 03 — Chemistry

Soil Chemistry &
Plant Metabolism

Chemistry underpins every process — from soil pH controlling nutrient availability, to NPK nutrients plants absorb, to the photosynthesis reaction converting light into glucose. These chemical processes explain why the right conditions are essential for growth.

🧪

Soil pH

We measured soil pH with pH paper. Most plants absorb nutrients best at pH 6–7. Below 6: toxic ions form. Above 7: phosphorus becomes insoluble. Write your measured pH values here.

🌱

NPK Nutrients

Nitrogen drives leaf growth; Phosphorus supports ATP and roots; Potassium regulates stomatal opening. All three were supplied equally to avoid nutrition being a confounding variable.

⚗️

Photosynthesis Rate

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂. Higher light intensity increases the rate of this reaction, producing more glucose for growth. Write your rate analysis here.
